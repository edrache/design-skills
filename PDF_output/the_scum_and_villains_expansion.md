### Page 1: The Hustler
---

The Hustler has much to hide and more to offer. Their power comes from their creativity, their connections, and their desire to finally belong.

*   **Explore** the tension between the gravity of a life of crime, and your desire for something more.
*   **Seek** opportunities to inspire your character to commit to people and causes from outside of their usual world.
*   **Remember** that character conflict is not player conflict; find ways to move towards shared narrative goals as characters disagree.

#### Spend a Token
*   Fight dirty and win.
*   Defy someone’s expectations of you.
*   Know what to say to get yourself out of trouble.
*   Reveal that you have a history with someone and call in a favor.
*   Appear at just the right moment to save a person or their plan.

#### Give a Token
*   Offer to lend someone a hand in exchange for a debt, and give them a token if they accept.
*   Ask a player or pillar “what can I give you to get what I want?” and give them a token if they accept it.
*   Ask a player or pillar “what could you say to encourage me to stand by you?” and give them a token if they share it.

#### Gain a Token
*   Get detained.
*   Provoke suspicion or distrust.
*   Refuse or disappear when someone needs your help.
*   Reveal that you have wronged someone in the past.
*   Say “what’s in it for me?”
*   Ask a player or pillar “how might I let you down in this moment?”

**Look:** Smug face, scarred face, pretty face, nervous face, young face.
**Voice:** Grating voice, nervous voice, quick speech, confident speech, “whoa, can we all just calm down for a second?”
**Clothing:** A many-pocketed vest, a leather jacket, stolen shipping company coveralls, tight pants, calf boots, goggles on your head, a bandana, a blaster in a thigh holster, a blaster hidden in your boot.
**Desires:** An escape from this livelihood, someone to take care of, a place that you feel wanted, the admiration of many, the chance for redemption, the opportunity to make a real difference.
**Assets:** A ship with a smuggling compartment, a crack shot, a winning smile, effortless lies, an inflated reputation, countless fake identities, favors to call in, black market connections, a contact everywhere.

#### Relationships (Select their trait)
*   The connected or analytical fixer who moves my goods
*   The friendly or cocky fugitive who relies on my connections
*   The innocent or trusting employee at a bar I frequent
*   My compassionate or troublemaker former crewmate I still trust
*   My maverick or faithful contact in the liberation
*   My cynical or maverick criminal rival from a job-went-south
*   The vengeful or commanding crime lord who hates you as much as you hate them
*   My independent or cautious ex who I worked with while we were together

**Starting Questions:**
*   What is something I secured for you, and why couldn’t you get it from anyone else?
*   What did I do recently that undermined your trust in me?
*   Why do you like working with me specifically?

### Page 2: The Mercenary
---

The Mercenary is a freelancer, who bears the scars of their countless jobs. Their power comes from their experience, their calloused skin, and their aching heart.

*   **Explore** the tension between the life you feel it’s too late escape, and the fate that will inevitably bring you something new.
*   **Put** your character in situations that confront their fate, and remember that change happens through failure and repetition.
*   **Remember** that you have your fate move in addition to the moves listed below!

#### Spend a Token
*   Win a fight you didn’t start.
*   See trouble coming just before it happens.
*   Appeal to your reputation to get what you want.
*   Say “I’ll hold them off.”
*   Say “I’ve seen this before.”

#### Give a Token
*   Tell someone why they’ll live to regret something they’re considering, and give them a token if they change their mind.
*   Ask a player or pillar “why do you see the potential for change in me?” and give them a token if they share it.

#### Gain a Token
*   Repeat an old mistake.
*   Start an ill-advised fight.
*   Choose the most violent option.
*   Tell someone a story about something you regret.
*   Say “as long as we get paid.”
*   Ask a player or pillar “what are you afraid I might do in this moment?”

**Look:** Weathered face, stony face, scarred face, unreadable face, aging face, masked face.
**Eyes:** Watchful eyes, tired eyes, impassive eyes, searching eyes, covered eyes, a thousand-yard stare.
**Clothing/Gear:** A chromasteel pauldron, a tall chassis, battered mandate armor, skintight pants, an ammo bandolier, a long blaster rifle, holsters everywhere, one more knife than expected, a hat pulled low, a floor length cape, heavy boots, a patch over an old employer’s insignia.
**Desires:** The support of a community, a sense of mercy, a cause worth committing to, the loyalty of a true friend, the courage to take a stand, someone to care for, the belief that there is more than this.
**Backgrounds:** Smuggling, robbery, surveillance, bodyguard, sabotage, extraction, extortion, bounty hunting, soldiery, kidnapping, assassination.

#### Fates & Fate Moves (pg. 16)
Guardianship, rebellion, faith, pacifism.

#### Relationships (Select their trait)
*   My cautious or trusting partner-in-crime
*   The friendly or connected barkeep who finds me jobs
*   The inventive or petulant tinkerer who services my gear
*   My rousing or faithful friend from a life before this one
*   The trusting or innocent target I abandoned a job to protect
*   The scheming or vengeful crime lord who wants my head
*   My passionate or austere ex who left me after learning what I’d done

**Starting Questions:**
*   What do you admire about me? Why haven’t you told me?
*   What is one job we did together? Did it go well?
*   Why would you rather work with me than another merc?

### Page 3: The Scum & The Villainy
---

#### The Scum
The galaxy is rife with desperation. On the margins of empire, those without options do what they must to make ends meet. These scum are everywhere—your friends, your peers, and even your rivals. Taken together, they are a force to be reckoned with, but their desperation can all too easily be used to divide them. They’re not always the most reliable bunch, but they’re who you have, and when the chips are down… they might just surprise you.

**Desires:** Redemption, notoriety, revenge, honor among thieves, reliable crew, escape from this livelihood, the jackpot, enough credits to get through the week.

**Moves:**
*   Reveal that people here have worked together before, and gain a token.
*   Have what someone needs, and gain a token.
*   Get someone involved in a dangerous scheme, and give them a token.
*   Come to someone’s aid when all seems lost, and give them a token.

**Advice:**
*   Show the solidarity and relationships that bloom even—and especially—in the face of desperation.
*   Create characters who are fallible, and give them the chance to prove themselves.
*   The scum might take work from those in the villainy, but they are never truly loyal to them. When creating characters to act as the villainy’s enforcers, attach hired freelancers to the scum, while grouping true believers and made men under the villainy.
*   Ask compelling questions and build on the answers.

#### The Villainy
The galaxy is rife with opportunity. On the margins of empire, where some see desperation, others see the potential for profit. These racketeers and warlords will kick you while you’re down, then charge you for the opportunity to stand. There are always credits to be made in their employment—and blaster bolts for those who dare to cross them.

**Desires:** Deniable assets, unscrupulous guns-for-hire, fealty, territory, tithes, monopoly, “respect”, uncomfortable bargains, strings to pull.

**Moves:**
*   Foreshadow a concealed threat, and gain a token.
*   Reveal that someone else is manipulating this situation, and gain a token.
*   Appeal to someone’s worst instincts, and give them a token.
*   Arrive at an inopportune time for someone, and give them a token.

**Advice:**
*   Explore how petty tyrants step into power vacuums and exploit desperation for profit and control.
*   Show how people struggle against tyranny in small ways all the time, and what consequences they face for doing so.
*   Ask compelling questions and build on the answers.

### Page 5: NPC Traits
---

![Traits Header](file:///Users/marek/OfflineDocuments/Repo/Antigravity/Design/PDF_output/images/page_5_img_1.png)

When creating an NPC, choose or roll two traits. You may use any combination of the 36 traits on pages 37-44 of the base book *Galactic 2e* and the 6 traits below (originally featured in *Going Rogue 2e*.)

The NPC gains the moves listed with their traits. Every NPC is grouped with a pillar, and their moves gain and give tokens from that pillar. See more information on NPC creation on page 7 of *Galactic 2e*.

1. **Cautious**
   *   Encourage deliberation about a decision, and gain a token.
   *   Remind someone of what they have to lose, and give them a token.
   *   Ask someone “Do you really think you can succeed?” and give them a token.

2. **Desperate**
   *   Say that you have nothing to lose, and gain a token.
   *   Betray someone out of necessity, and give them a token.
   *   Ask someone to help you even though you have nothing to offer them, and give them a token.

3. **Independent**
   *   Say “I’ll do it myself” and gain a token.
   *   Refuse someone’s offer of help, and give them a token.
   *   Show up to assist someone without ever having been asked, and give them a token.

4. **Petulant**
   *   Complain, and gain a token.
   *   Narc on someone, and give them a token.
   *   Have an explosive outburst at someone, and give them a token.

5. **Trusting**
   *   Ignore a warning sign, and gain a token.
   *   Follow someone into a risky situation, and give them a token.
   *   Ask someone “What do we do now?” and give them a token.

6. **Vengeful**
   *   Reference a time you were wronged, and gain a token.
   *   Arrive at the worst time for someone, and give them a token.
   *   Declare that you will never forgive someone, and give them a token.

> [!NOTE]
> If you’re currently filling out your playbook and trying to find more details about the two traits associated with each of your relationships, many of those traits cannot be found above, as most traits come from the base game. See pages 37-44 of the base book (*Galactic 2e* by Riley Rethal) for the other 36 traits.

#### Pillar Moves Quick Scan
*Always read the full pillar page before using this reference.*

**The Scum**
*   Reveal that people here have worked together before, and gain a token.
*   Have what someone needs, and gain a token.
*   Get someone involved in a dangerous scheme, and give them a token.
*   Come to someone’s aid when all seems lost, and give them a token.

**The Villainy**
*   Foreshadow a concealed threat, and gain a token.
*   Reveal that someone else is manipulating this situation, and gain a token.
*   Appeal to someone’s worst instincts, and give them a token.
*   Arrive at an inopportune time for someone, and give them a token.

**The Liberation**
*   Complicate someone’s vision of freedom, and gain a token.
*   Offer help and solidarity, with or without a cost. If they accept, give them a token.
*   Introduce dangerous plans and ask others to participate. If they do, give them a token.
*   Ask “What does a better world look like to you?”, and give them a token if they answer.

**The Empire**
*   Intercept information or supplies, and give a token to whom you’ve taken from.
*   Put someone in immediate danger, and give them a token.
*   Foreshadow a larger threat, and gain a token.

**The Mystery**
*   Invoke awe, and gain a token.
*   Reveal an old secret or new truth to someone, and give them a token.
*   Create an unexpected connection between people, and give them a token.

#### Recommended Setup Order
*Be sure to read the full instructions and tips on pages 4-9 before play.*

1. Read aloud the descriptions of the Scum and the Villainy pillars, then select their desires.
2. Choose playbooks and answer the starting prompts for your playbooks (discussing as you go) and stopping just before the relationships.
3. Go around the table, sharing your prompt choices aloud.
4. Complete the remaining pillars from *Galactic 2e*.
5. Collaboratively complete the relationships and ask left & right questions on your playbook.
